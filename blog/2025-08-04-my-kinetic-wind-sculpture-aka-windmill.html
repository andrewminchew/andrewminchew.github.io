Building a windmill to keep in my yard

It’s hard to pin down exactly what motivated me to make this windmill. Some confluence of a number of factors I’m aware of:

  • I love the Eames film Solar Do-Nothing Machine
  • My grandpa made a windmill out of popsicle sticks when I was a kid and I thought it was really cool.
  • I made my own popsicle stick windmill a few years back, but it’s since been lost to decay and a move.
  • I love things that make the weather tangible or translate it into another sense: rain gauges, kinetic sculptures, wind chimes, the traditional Japanese shishi-odoshi (bamboo deer scarer), those little solar powered bobble heads that you put in your window sill or car dashboard...
  • We got a 3d printer recently and I’ve been itching for a new project to model.

So I started sketching and got my ideas together.

Once I had my head wrapped around it, I got into OnShape, started printing parts and assembling. I had recently finished another project where I started using bamboo skewers as a supplementary construction material, and I wanted to make something a little bigger using the same concept.
